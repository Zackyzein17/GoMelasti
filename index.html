About the Beach

A Sacred Shore at the Edge of Bali

Pantai Melasti (Melasti Beach) sits at the very southern tip of the Bukit Peninsula, marking the southernmost reach of Bali. Its name is drawn from the sacred Balinese Melasti ritual — a ceremony of purification performed before Nyepi Day — a fitting name for a beach that cleanses the soul with every visit.


Once a hidden gem concealed behind dramatic limestone cliffs with difficult access, Melasti has since blossomed into one of Bali's most beloved stretches of coastline. Today, a scenic paved road winds through the cliffs, opening onto a broad sweep of powder-white sand, crystal-clear turquoise water, and the endless blue of the Indian Ocean.


Unlike most Uluwatu beaches accessible only by steep staircases, you can drive right to the beachfront — a rare convenience that doesn't diminish its wild beauty one bit.

What to Do

Attractions & Activities

From crystal-clear swimming to golden-hour photography, Melasti rewards every kind of visitor with its natural drama and laid-back charm.

🏊

Water Activity

Swimming & Floating

The calm, turquoise waters of Melasti are ideal for swimming. During low tide, natural rock pools form near the shore — perfect for sitting and soaking in the scenery. The gentle waves make it safe for most visitors.

📸

Photography

Cliff & Scenic Photography

The dramatic limestone cliffs framing the beach are iconic. The winding road down offers sweeping panoramic shots, while sunrise and sunset paint the cliffs in amber and gold. Popular for pre-wedding and professional photoshoots.

🍹

Beach Club

Beach Clubs & Sunbathing

Rent a sunbed (around IDR 200,000 for two) and settle in for the day. Popular clubs include Palmilla Bali, White Rock, Sundays, Klive, and Tropical Temptation — each offering pools, cocktails, and ocean views.

🌅

Sunset Experience

Golden Hour & Sunsets

Melt into the magic hour as the sun dips behind the Indian Ocean. The cliffs glow amber, the water turns to liquid gold, and the beach clubs fill with the soft hum of music and conversation. One of Bali's finest sunset vantage points.

🍜

Local Food

Warungs & Local Bites

Several local warungs (small family eateries) serve fresh Balinese dishes, cold coconut drinks, and ice-cold Bintang beer right on the beachfront — a perfect affordable alternative to the beach clubs.

🚶

Exploration

Cliff Walk & Scenic Drive

The scenic access road that winds down through the limestone cliffs is an attraction in itself. Stop at the viewpoints on the way down for breathtaking aerial perspectives of the beach and coastline below.

Facilities

Amenities & Facilities

Melasti is one of Bali's most well-equipped beaches. Whether you're staying all day or just stopping for a dip, you'll find everything you need within easy reach.

Basic Facilities

  • 🚻
    Public Toilets Clean, well-maintained public restrooms available near the main parking area. Typically free or very low cost.
  • 🚿
    Shower Facilities Outdoor showers located near the parking zone for rinsing off sand and saltwater after swimming.
  • 👗
    Changing Rooms Changing facilities available on-site for swimmers and visitors who need to freshen up.
  • 🅿️
    Parking Area Large, well-organised parking lot for both motorbikes and cars directly at the beachfront. Entrance fee ~IDR 33,000 including parking.
  • 🏪
    Convenience Store On-site convenience store selling snacks, drinks, sunscreen, and beach essentials.

Food, Drink & Relaxation

  • 🍜
    Local Warungs Several family-run warungs serve affordable Balinese and Indonesian food including nasi goreng, grilled seafood, and fresh coconut drinks.
  • 🍸
    Beach Clubs & Bars Over a dozen beach clubs (Palmilla, White Rock, Sundays, Klive, Tropical Temptation) with restaurants, bars, pools, and premium loungers.
  • ☂️
    Sun Loungers & Umbrellas Rentable sun loungers and umbrellas along the beach. Typically IDR 200,000 for a pair of sunbeds.
  • 🌐
    WiFi (Beach Clubs) Most beach clubs offer complimentary WiFi for their guests. Connectivity is generally good throughout the area.
  • ♿
    Easy Access — No Stairs Unlike most Uluwatu beaches, Melasti is fully accessible by car or motorbike right to the beachfront — no steep staircases required.

Getting There

Location & Directions

Melasti Beach is located in the Ungasan area of the Bukit Peninsula, on Bali's southern coast. Look for the signposted turnoff near Ungasan village, then follow the winding scenic road down through the cliffs to the beach.

  • ✈️
    From Ngurah Rai Airport ~30–45 minutes by car or scooter. Head south toward Uluwatu via the coastal toll road.
  • 🏖️
    From Kuta / Legian ~45–60 minutes by scooter or taxi. Take Jalan Uluwatu toward Ungasan.
  • 🌴
    From Seminyak / Canggu ~1.5 to 2 hours. Head south through Kuta, then continue toward Bukit Peninsula.
  • 🛕
    From Uluwatu Temple ~10–15 minutes. Head east from the temple toward Ungasan, follow signs to Melasti Beach.
  • 🗺️
    Coordinates 8°52'25.1"S 115°10'31.2"E — search "Pantai Melasti Ungasan" on Google Maps for turn-by-turn directions.

Visitor Tips

Make the Most of Your Visit

A few local tips to help you have the perfect Melasti Beach day.

🌤️ Best Time to Visit

Come during the dry season (April–October) for the clearest skies and calmest waters. Early morning visits mean fewer crowds and stunning golden light on the cliffs.

💰 Bring Cash

While beach clubs may accept cards, local warungs and many facilities operate on a cash basis. Bring Indonesian Rupiah (IDR) to cover entrance fees, parking, food, and rentals.

🧴 Pack Essentials

Bring reef-safe sunscreen, swimwear, a towel, and plenty of water. A small bag of cash, your camera, and a change of clothes will set you up perfectly for a full beach day.

🛵 Getting Around

Renting a scooter is the most popular and affordable way to reach Melasti from most parts of Bali. Taxis and ride-hailing apps like Gojek and Grab are also reliable options.

🌊 Swimming Safety

Melasti is one of the safest swimming beaches in Uluwatu thanks to calm conditions, especially during dry season. Always check conditions on arrival and swim between safe zones.

📸 Photography Permit

Planning a professional or pre-wedding photoshoot? A permit (IDR 500,000) is required. Contact the beach management on arrival or arrange in advance for a seamless session.
